## **Topic**
**Agentic AI: The Next Big Shift in Software Development**

## **Overview** 
Agentic AI (also call AI Agents) refers to AI that can plan, decide, and perform tasks independently throughout the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). This means that Agentic AI is moving from being a reactive assistant to being a proactive partner in the software development process. 
Analysts have noted that AI Agents can anticipate developers' requirements, optimize applications in real-time, and perform multi-step processes without being prompted each step of the way. At the same time, Gartner has indicated a broader transition to AI-native software engineering, where 90% of enterprise software engineers will use AI code assistants by 2028 compared to less than 14%in early 2024, thus changing the human’s role in software development from implementation to orchestration and oversight. 
## **Four reasons that make Agentic AI timely**
1. Maturity of tools:  AI-enhanced coding, testing, and refactoring has become mainstream and is creating the foundations for autonomous workflows.
2. Pull from enterprises: There is pressure for quicker cycle times and lower cognitive load; agentic systems can automate repetitive coding tasks and perform routine overrides.
3. Market momentum: The Agentic AI market is estimated to be at $47B market by 2027, demonstrating the acceleration of capital for investment and deployment in this area.
4. Integration into platforms: GenAI capabilities are being embedded into internal developer platforms, allowing for Agentic systems to be discovered, governed, and audited.

Agentic AI will perform actions across all stages of the SDLC. In the planning phase, Agentic AI will expand user stories into task graphs, open issues that help priority tasks, track completion, and aid in work structure. 
During the implementation phase, Agentic AI can create scaffolding of modules, generate test cases, enforce linting and security policies, and summarize code changes. In the verification and operations phases of the SDLC, agents will run test suites and determine if any tests that were executing caused flakiness, prioritize failed tests, enhance performance via telemetry, and trigger safe rollbacks if needed.

## **Benefits**

Depending on the team they report or target , the AI Agent enhances frequent deployments, and fewer defects that escape, as it eliminates toil and continually check for issues on behalf of developers, refocus efforts on architecture, risk trade-offs, and overall system design. ​

As a result, developers experience improved developer experience (fewer context switches) and more consistent application of common standards at scale.

## **Risks and Guardrails**
As autonomy increases, so does the blast radius of errors in the models used. Therefore, organizations can mitigate these risks by implementing human approval on sensitive automated actions; enforced least-privilege credentials; policy as code checks during the continuous integration process; and audit logs to track agent actions. In addition, teams should define clear ‘red lines’ for those actions that remain under human control, such as schema change requests, etc.
